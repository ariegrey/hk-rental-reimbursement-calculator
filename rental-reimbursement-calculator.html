Hong Kong Rental Reimbursement – Assessable Income Estimator

This page gives a rough estimation of how a rental reimbursement scheme (treated as a place of residence with Rental Value, “RV”) may change your assessable income under Hong Kong salaries tax. It does not replace professional tax advice.

The figures are for one Hong Kong year of assessment (from 1 April to 31 March of the following year). Employment income typically includes salary, bonus, commissions, vested share awards, stock option gains, and benefits in kind arising from your employment.

The ultimate goal of this tool is to compare the assessable income under Scenario A (no rental reimbursement) and Scenario B (with rental reimbursement) and to show which scenario is more favourable in terms of lower assessable income.

3. Evidence and IRD examples

For the rental reimbursement scheme to be accepted as a place of residence provided (and not as a pure cash allowance), the employer should keep proper evidence such as: stamped tenancy agreement, rent receipts and internal records showing that normal landlord–tenant formalities are observed.

The treatment of Rental Value and “rent suffered” in this tool is broadly aligned with the Inland Revenue Department’s examples in the pamphlet “How to tax the provision of a place of residence to the employee” , including situations where the employee bears part of the rent and the taxable benefit effectively reflects RV minus rent paid by the employee.

Important:
• This is a simplified estimator focusing on assessable income only. It does not calculate actual salaries tax or personal allowances.
• The scheme only works as intended if the Inland Revenue Department accepts that a place of residence is provided and the employer keeps sufficient documentation (lease, receipts, internal policy, etc.).
• Actual tax treatment depends on IRD’s final assessment and your individual circumstances.
