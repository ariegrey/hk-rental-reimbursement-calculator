# Hong Kong Rental Reimbursement Calculator

A free public calculator that helps users compare assessable income under two scenarios in Hong Kong.

- **Scenario A:** No rental reimbursement
- **Scenario B:** With rental reimbursement using a simplified Rental Value (RV) approach

This tool is designed as a simple educational estimator for employees, HR teams, and anyone who wants a quick comparison of whether a rental reimbursement arrangement may be more favourable from an assessable income perspective under Hong Kong salaries tax rules.

## Live calculator

Use the calculator here:

**Website:** <https://ariegrey.github.io/hk-rental-reimbursement-calculator/>

## What this calculator does

The calculator compares assessable income on a simplified basis for one Hong Kong year of assessment, which runs from **1 April to 31 March**.

It allows users to enter:

- Annual employment income
- Monthly actual rent paid
- Monthly employer housing reimbursement
- Type of accommodation for Rental Value percentage

It then shows:

- Assessable income under **Scenario A**
- Assessable income under **Scenario B**
- The more favourable scenario based on the lower assessable income

The logic is based on Hong Kong Inland Revenue Department guidance on housing benefits, including Rental Value percentages and examples where part of the rent is borne by the employee.

## Key assumptions

This calculator is intentionally simplified.

It assumes:

- One Hong Kong employer only
- No personal allowances
- No deductions
- No final salaries tax calculation
- The rental reimbursement arrangement is accepted as a **place of residence provided** rather than a cash allowance

Because of these assumptions, this tool should be used for **illustration and comparison only**, not as tax advice.

## Important note on evidence

For a rental reimbursement arrangement to work as intended, the employer should keep proper supporting records such as tenancy agreements, rent receipts, and related internal documentation showing that the arrangement is a genuine housing benefit arrangement rather than a pure cash payment.

Users should refer to the IRD pamphlet and official Hong Kong government guidance for the underlying rules and examples.
